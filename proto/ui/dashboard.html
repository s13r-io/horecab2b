How Order Quantity is Calculated

Daily Use = weighted average of last 7 days consumption (recent days weighted higher), adjusted for weekends and upcoming events

Days of Stock = current stock on hand / daily use

Lead Time = days until earliest vendor delivery, based on each vendor's order cutoff time (if cutoff is missed today, delivery shifts by a day)

Order Qty = (daily use x coverage days) - current stock on hand

Where coverage days = lead time + lead time (next cycle) + 1 day safety buffer

MOQ = if calculated order qty is below a vendor's minimum order quantity, it is rounded up to meet the MOQ

Example: Chicken Breast uses 11.5 kg/day, lead time is 1 day. Coverage = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3 days. Target stock = 11.5 x 3 = 34.5 kg. Current stock = 2.0 kg. Order = 34.5 - 2.0 = 32.5 kg.

How Vendor is Selected

Effective Cost = total cost x (1 - credit days x 0.1% per day)

Vendors offering longer credit terms get a lower effective cost — reflecting the real value of holding working capital longer

Tiebreaker = if two vendors have the same effective cost, the more reliable one is preferred

Split Ordering (Urgency)

When stock won't last until the preferred (cheapest) vendor can deliver, the order is split:

Bridge Order = small urgent order from the fastest available vendor, just enough to cover the gap until the preferred vendor delivers

Main Order = total forecast quantity minus the bridge quantity, placed with the preferred vendor at a better price

Bridge qty accounts for stock remaining at the time the bridge delivery arrives

Example: Chicken Breast — preferred vendor (Local Meat, Rs.295/kg) missed cutoff, delivers in 2 days. Stock is 2 kg (0.2 days). Hyperpure (Rs.305/kg) cutoff still open, delivers tomorrow. Bridge: 11.5 kg from Hyperpure. Main: 32.4 - 11.5 = 20.9 kg from Local Meat. Saves Rs.209 vs ordering everything from Hyperpure.
